I am a Ph.D. candidate in Transportation Engineering at [Southeast University (SEU)](https://www.seu.edu.cn/english/) in Nanjing, China. Currently, I am a Visiting Scholar at the Department of Civil, Environmental, and Geo- Engineering (CEGE) at the **University of Minnesota, Twin Cities (UMN)** (Jan 2025 - Present).

My research broadly encompasses **transportation network resilience**, **traffic flow prediction**, and **smart logistics optimization**. I am particularly interested in optimization analysis of urban road networks under natural disasters (such as floods) and other extreme weather disruptions.

My current work focuses on developing advanced heuristic algorithms, such as Adaptive Large Neighborhood Search (ALNS) and Benders Decomposition, to solve complex vehicle routing challenges like preemptive snow removal routing. I also integrate continuous/discrete network design (CNDP/DNDP) and high-performance simulation tools like SUMO and AutoDL to evaluate large-scale traffic states and enhance urban infrastructure robustness. I am dedicated to publishing high-quality research in top-tier journals such as *Transportation Research Part B/E*.
